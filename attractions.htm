Whale Shark Interaction

Experience the awe-inspiring moment of swimming with whale sharks. These gentle giants are protected, so it’s crucial to follow the guidelines to ensure a safe and eco-friendly encounter.

Location

Barangay Tan-awan, Oslob, Cebu

Rules & Guidelines

  • No touching or riding the whale sharks.
  • Keep a distance of at least 4 meters.
  • Do not use flash photography.
  • Wear only reef-safe sunscreen.

Gear & Eco-Friendly Tips

  • Snorkel, mask, and life vest are usually provided on-site.
  • Avoid single-use plastics; bring a reusable water bottle.
  • Respect the marine life and do not feed them outside designated feeding schedules.

Tumalog Falls

Tumalog Falls is a picturesque waterfall nestled in a lush forest. Its unique curtain-like cascade and shallow basin make it perfect for a relaxing dip.

Location

Barangay Luka, Oslob, Cebu

Rules & Guidelines

  • No littering. Use designated trash bins.
  • Swimming is allowed, but always follow local safety instructions.
  • Keep noise to a minimum to preserve the natural ambiance.

Gear & Eco-Friendly Tips

  • Wear water shoes for better traction on slippery rocks.
  • Bring a reusable water bottle to stay hydrated.
  • Leave no trace: take all belongings and trash with you.

Cuartel Ruins

The Cuartel Ruins stand as a historical landmark from the Spanish colonial era. Its unfinished walls and coral-stone construction give a glimpse into Oslob’s storied past.

Location

Fronting the Municipal Hall, Poblacion, Oslob, Cebu

Rules & Guidelines

  • Respect the site; do not climb on the walls.
  • Photography is allowed, but avoid damaging the structure.

Eco-Friendly Tips

  • Carry a reusable water bottle to minimize plastic waste.
  • Support local vendors to help sustain the community.

Sumilon Island

Famous for its shifting white sandbar and crystal-clear waters, Sumilon Island is a haven for snorkeling, kayaking, and beach relaxation.

Location

Off the coast of Oslob, Cebu (Boat transfer from mainland)

Rules & Guidelines

  • Book boat transfers with accredited operators.
  • No littering or collecting marine life (shells, corals, etc.).

Gear & Eco-Friendly Tips

  • Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect marine ecosystems.
  • Use reusable containers for food and drinks.
  • Respect marine life while snorkeling or diving.
